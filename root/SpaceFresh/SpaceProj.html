Atlas took us to orbit

As the excitement for the moon program diminished during the 1970's budgets were cut and systems shutdown. NASA had plans to go to Mars during the Apollo program. But with budget cuts and the loss of incentive due to a lack of competition. All programs were cut.

John Glenn piloted the first American orbit of earth. Flying on top of the Atlas D, a modified Atlas 1, developed in 1957. As mentioned before the “space race” was a political beast. Astronaut Glenn watched the first 5 Atlas D rockets explode during test launches. Because having command of the High Ground was so important, Glenn rode the Atlas D to the first American orbit of the Earth. The Astronauts got to name their capsules. Glenn named his Friendship 7 to extend a hand to the Russians, and to try and stimulate team spirit among the Mercury astronaut team. There were 7 Mercury Pilots.
